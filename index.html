DILG orders local officials to stay in post as typhoon Uwan enters enter PAR




MANILA — The Department of the Interior and Local Government has directed all local chief executives to suspend foreign travel and to stay in their respective jurisdictions to lead disaster preparedness and response efforts as typhoon Uwan enters the Philippine Area of Responsibility.
Local Government Secretary Jonvic Remulla issued the order on Saturday, emphasizing the need for “full leadership presence” in all provinces, cities, municipalities, and barangays while the country braces for the effects of the typhoon.
The directive comes after President Ferdinand Marcos Jr. placed the country under a state of national calamity, following forecasts that Typhoon Uwan could bring heavy rains, strong winds, and possible flooding and landslides in several regions.
Authorities continue to urge the public to stay alert, monitor official advisories, and cooperate with local authorities as the nation braces for the impact of the storm.
